Treatments for lymphoedema of the legs

  • Keep legs covered when gardening to prevent any cuts or scratches.
  • Do not walk around barefoot. You may step on something or stub your foot.
  • Avoid clothes which are tight around the groin or abdomen. They may restrict fluid flow.
  • Always dry between toes thoroughly after washing to prevent fungal infection.
  • Try to wear correct fitting shoes which are well supporting. Avoid high heeled and low cut shoes.

You have been given wraps to reduce or manage your swollen leg. There are different makes of Velcro wraps. Basically, they are very much the same.

Advice for using your wraps

  • We always advise you wear toe caps underneath your foot wrap.
  • Never wear the leg wrap without the foot wrap unless you have been given a liner which has compression in the foot area.
  • Apply the foot piece and then the leg piece/s working up the leg.
  • Wear the wraps as advised by your therapist
  • Regularly adjust the wrap throughout the day as it may become loose as swelling reduces.
  • Adjust or loosen the wrap if it feels uncomfortable or you get pins and needles or blue toes.
  • Remove the wrap if you have a sudden onset of shortness of breathe
  • Remove the wrap if you develop any skin allergy to the material
