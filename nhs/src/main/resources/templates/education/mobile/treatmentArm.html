Treatments for lymphedema of the legs

  • Wear rubber gloves when washing up or using cleaning materials - as they can irritate the skin.
  • Wear gloves when gardening to prevent any cuts or scratches to your arm or hand.
  • Use a thimble when sewing.
  • Try to avoid having your blood pressure checked on a swollen arm.
  • Do not wear tight clothing, i.e. tight bra straps, t-shirts or vests with tight arm holes as they can restrict fluid flow. This can also apply to watches and jewellery such as rings or bracelets.
  • Avoid carrying or lifting heavy objects - including full shopping bags - as this puts strain on your arm and could lead to an increase in swelling.

You have been given wraps to reduce or manage your swollen arm. There are different makes of Velcro wraps. Basically, they are very much the same.

Advice for using your wraps

  • We always advise you wear toe caps underneath your foot wrap.
  • Never wear the leg wrap without the foot wrap unless you have been given a liner which has compression in the foot area.
  • Apply the foot piece and then the leg piece/s working up the leg.
  • Wear the wraps as advised by your therapist
  • Regularly adjust the wrap throughout the day as it may become loose as swelling reduces.
  • Adjust or loosen the wrap if it feels uncomfortable or you get pins and needles or blue toes.
  • Remove the wrap if you have a sudden onset of shortness of breathe
  • Remove the wrap if you develop any skin allergy to the material

Advice for using your wraps

  • We always advise you wear a glove underneath your hand piece
  • Never wear the arm wrap without the hand wrap unless you have been advised to do so
  • Ensure there is no gap between the hand and arm pieces at the wrist
  • Wear the wraps as advised by your therapist
  • Regularly adjust the wrap throughout the day as it may become loose as swelling reduces.
  • Adjust or loosen the wrap if it feels uncomfortable or you get pins and needles or blue fingers.
  • Remove the wrap if you have a sudden onset of shortness of breathe
  • Remove the wrap if you develop any skin allergy to the material

Manual and simple lymphatic drainage is a gentle massage where the skin is stretched along the lymphatic pathway. Regular simple Lymphatic Drainage can help move extra fluid from swollen areas damaged by cancer treatments to areas where the lymphatic system is working well.
